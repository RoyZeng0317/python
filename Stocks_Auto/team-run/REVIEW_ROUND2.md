## Issues (severity, description, suggested fix)

No BLOCKER or MAJOR issues found this round. Two MINOR observations below, neither blocks PASS.

### MINOR — `grouping.py`'s first-match-wins design can attribute broad market-wrap articles to a single company mentioned in a list

Running the pipeline myself produced a new single-article group `"3711 日月光投控"` for the article *"《台股盤後》午盤拉逾千點 終場跌642點收44737點"* — a general market-close roundup that never mentions 日月光投控 in its title. I re-fetched the live Yahoo article body directly and confirmed the match is **not** a false positive in the Round-1 sense (wrong entity / unrelated topic): the body genuinely says "...智邦、國巨*、鴻勁、日月光投控、聯發科等電子權值股普遍承壓" — 日月光投控 is a real company named in that sentence. But the article's actual subject is the whole market, and it happens to name several other weighted stocks too; `group()` breaks on the first matching keyword in its longest-first lookup order and puts the whole article in exactly one group. This is a pre-existing design property (not something Round 1 introduced or regressed) and is a legitimate "long name substring match", which the Round 1 fix deliberately did not restrict (correctly — restricting it would cut recall for the vast majority of normal-length company names that are the actual subject of an article). Not a regression, not a new false-positive class, and arguably still useful context for someone tracking 日月光投控's news feed. No fix required for v1; if it recurs as a nuisance, a future iteration could require the matched keyword to appear within the first N% of the body or restrict multi-stock roundup articles (e.g. detect "XX等電子權值股" list patterns) to the 大盤/未分類 bucket, but this is optional polish, not correctness. [Medium confidence this is acceptable — reasoning is sound but it does illustrate the boundary of what "signal-based" matching can catch.]

### MINOR — `COMMON_WORD_COLLISION_KEYWORDS`'s "known common-word short-name" sublist is honestly unbounded, but this is an acceptable v1 trade-off, not "the feature is untrustworthy"

BUILD_LOG.md's own framing ("誠實陳述...本質上無法窮舉") is accurate and I agree with it, but I want to give an independent judgment rather than just accept it: this list only needs to catch cases where a company's *official short name* collides with a common word/place name/well-known brand — a narrow, discoverable category, not an open-ended NLP problem. The structural defenses around it (title-match-not-enough for collision-prone keywords, code-proximity requirement, exclusion-clause detection) are signal-based rather than a blind stopword list, which is exactly the kind of fix the Round 1 review asked for (see REVIEW.md's suggested fix #2). Two of the six specific collision cases in the list (三星, related to the SK Hynix/Samsung ADR story) were verified by me independently landing correctly in 大盤/未分類 on a **live, freshly-fetched** article (see cross-checks below) — this is not just the Builder's own claim. Given PLAN.md's stop-condition framing ("v1好到可以用的定義", not "perfect"), and that every single-article group I was able to independently audit across two separate pipeline runs (21 groups total) was a true positive, I judge this residual risk acceptable for v1 and not a reason to withhold PASS. It should stay on the team's radar as a "living list" that needs occasional additions.
